📝 Exam Day Experience

I took my CCST exam at a test center - and honestly, it was my first time ever doing something like that. I was a mix of nervous and curious, wondering what the place would even look like.

It turned out the exam center was super organized. I got there about 30 minutes early, and luckily, the examiner was chill - he let me sign in ahead of time. Even though I was ready, I still felt pretty nervous (and yep, I ended up going to the washroom right before starting, so entered exactly at my scheduled time

There weren't any surprises, but just a heads-up:
You'll go through a strict check - ears, pockets, sleeves, the whole deal.

You're monitored by video and audio the entire time, which honestly made it feel more serious - like a rea/ exam.

•You can bring water, but no noise allowed - the room needs to stay silent.

·If you use a locker, keep your ID and key card with you every time you leave or re-enter the test room.

Oh, and they provided noise-canceling headphones, which were surprisingly good. Helped me stay focused the whole time.

I actually finished 18 minutes early - not because I rushed, but because the exam wasn't too hard, especially if vou
prepared well.

That's pretty much it. Nothing crazy, no magic - just staying consistent and learning at my own pace.
If I can do it while juggling life and work, you definitely can too.
