The **Mega Lab** is a 14-phase build of an enterprise network. Each phase reuses the config from the last, so the topology grows as you go.

## Implementation Roadmap

### Section 1: The Layer 2 Core
1.  **[Phase 1: Network Fundamentals](/labs/mega-lab/phase-1-network-fundamentals--basic-connectivity-and-osi/)** - Basic Connectivity & OSI.
2.  **[Phase 2: IPv4/v6 Addressing](/labs/mega-lab/phase-2-ipv4ipv6-addressing-and-subnetting/)** - Addressing & Subnetting.
3.  **[Phase 3: VLANs & Trunking](/labs/mega-lab/phase-3-vlans-trunking-and-vtp-layer-2-segmentation/)** - Layer 2 Segmentation.
4.  **[Phase 4: Inter-VLAN Routing](/labs/mega-lab/phase-4-inter-vlan-routing-router-on-a-stick/)** - Router-on-a-Stick.
5.  **[Phase 5: STP & EtherChannel](/labs/mega-lab/phase-5--spanning-tree-rstp-and-etherchannel/)** - Redundancy & Aggregation.

### Section 2: Layer 3 Routing
6.  **[Phase 6: Static Routing](/labs/mega-lab/phase-6-static-routing-multi-router-network/)** - Multi-Router Network.
7.  **[Phase 7: Dynamic Routing – RIP](/labs/mega-lab/phase-7-dynamic-routing--rip/)** - Basic Distance Vector.
8.  **[Phase 8: Dynamic Routing – OSPF](/labs/mega-lab/phase-8-dynamic-routing-ospf/)** - Link-State Routing.

### Section 3: Services & Security
9.  **[Phase 9: ACLs & NAT](/labs/mega-lab/phase-9--access-control-lists-acls-and-nat/)** - Traffic Control & Internet Access.
10. **[Phase 10: IP Services](/labs/mega-lab/phase-10-dhcp-dns-snmp-and-other-ip-services/)** - DHCP, DNS, SNMP & NTP.
11. **[Phase 11: WAN Links](/labs/mega-lab/phase-11-wan-links-hdlc-and-ppp/)** - HDLC and PPP.
12. **[Phase 12: Layer 2 Security](/labs/mega-lab/phase-12-layer-2-security-port-security-dhcp-snooping-dai/)** - Port-Security & DAI.

### Section 4: Management & Beyond
13. **[Phase 13: Automation](/labs/mega-lab/phase-13-device-management-and-automation/)** - Device Management & APIs.
14. **[Phase 14: Final Integration](/labs/mega-lab/phase-14/)** - End-to-End Enterprise Connectivity.

---

> [!IMPORTANT]
> It is recommended to complete these phases in order, as each one relies on the configuration and connectivity established in the previous step.
