## Review and Exam Preparation Tips

- **Reflect on Each Phase:** After finishing a phase, use `show` and `debug` commands to verify all configurations. Explain in writing what each setting does (e.g. “`switchport port-security` prevents additional MACs on this port”). This deepens understanding.
    
- **Save Snapshots:** In Packet Tracer, use **File > Save As** to create versioned files (e.g. Phase1.pkt, Phase2.pkt, …). You can reload any phase’s file to review or if you need to revert changes.
    
- **Troubleshoot Intentionally:** Each phase includes deliberate misconfigurations. Practice diagnosing by disabling interfaces, altering configs, or using wrong credentials. Troubleshooting is a big part of CCNA.
    
- **Track Your Progress:** Keep a lab diary or checklist. For example: “Phase 5 completed – STP root bridge set; EtherChannel up; all VLANs reachable.” Use CCNA exam topic lists to ensure every concept is covered.
    
- **Use Packet Tracer Features:** Experiment with _Simulation Mode_ to step through packets, and the built-in _Physical Workspace_ to see device lights and connections.
    
- **Exam Focus:** Align each lab task with CCNA objectives (cited above). After the lab, quiz yourself: e.g. “How does OSPF elect a DR/BDR?” or “What are 5 steps of TCP three-way handshake?”. Hands-on practice helps solidify theory.
    

When you finish, you’ll have a full network in Packet Tracer covering every CCNA 200-301 topic: VLANs, security, routing, WAN links, and automation. Each phase depends on the one before it, so by Phase 13 the whole topology works end to end.
